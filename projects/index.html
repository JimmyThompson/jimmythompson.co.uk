Projects

Hacked @ the O2

Participant at Hacked - at the O2 in London - in July, 2013. Worked with Asad Haider, Benjamin Dale and Libbie Chesters to create a Hackathon Management System with accompanying Android application in 24 hours using PHP, CodeIgniter and Java.

Hack Manchester 2012

Participant at Hack Manchester 2012, 24-hour coding competition with Asad Haider. Entry was a social networking/planning tool for people going travelling which plotted social network data on the map plus allows for planning meetings with other travellers. Runner up in the Laterooms challenge.

Bloodline Champions Wiki

In 2011 I launched a wiki used to cover information surrounding the up-and-coming arena game Bloodline Champions. This meant creating an environment, using MediaWiki plus various plugins, for people to create and collaborate on information regarding the game and the surrounding eSports scene which was developing around it. Furthermore, as the site became more popular I had to maintain the Debian server which the site was running on.

DevXS

DevXS 2011 group projects at Lincoln University. Built a community powered library application, written in PHP & MySQL in 24 hours. Students could find out the more highly rated books as recommended by their fellow students on their course, comparing them to the official reading list. Winner of the LiSC award for socially active computing.

PlayerPowered

A small gaming blog managed by myself and some guys I knew online back in 2011. The main selling point was that the readers got to decide what was written about next. Working with Wordpress and some custom tools, the site carried on for several months before it was closed due to a lack of time to write, what with University etc.

GamerFM

Internet radio aimed at people playing online games. Founded in 2008, it included the 24/7 streaming of music over an Icecast server combined with a complete custom website solution written in PHP using MySQL. Technical work included developing a brand, website and various tools so people could listen as well as interact with the people live on-air. Aside from that, most of the work was in developing relationships with gaming communities as well as the day-to-day management of the radio itself.

The radio grew to approximately 400-500 concurrent listeners and just over 10,000 active forum users, maintained by up to 30 volunteer staff. Said staff also went on to broadcast for GamerFM live at the Insomnia Gaming Festival in Summer 2010.
